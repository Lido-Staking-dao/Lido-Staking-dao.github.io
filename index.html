What Is Lido?

Lido is a liquid staking middleware protocol that lets users participate in Ethereum staking without locking assets or running validator infrastructure.

When you stake ETH through Lido, you receive stETH — a rebasing token whose balance updates daily to reflect staking rewards. Hold stETH in your wallet and watch your balance grow, or deploy it across DeFi protocols for additional yield.

Governed by the Lido DAO through the LDO token, the protocol operates a curated set of professional node operators and has been live since December 2020.

Lido DAO Governance

Lido is governed by the Lido DAO — LDO token holders vote on protocol parameters, node operator selection, and treasury allocation.

LDO Token

The LDO governance token enables holders to vote on protocol proposals via Snapshot and on-chain through Aragon. One LDO equals one vote on all DAO decisions.

Node Operator Selection

The DAO curates and maintains the set of node operators that run validators. Operators are selected based on track record, infrastructure quality, and geographic diversity.

Fee Structure

Lido charges a 10% fee on staking rewards, split equally: 5% to node operators for running infrastructure and 5% to the Lido DAO treasury for protocol development.

Dual Governance

A new mechanism giving stETH holders veto power over DAO decisions that affect their staked assets. Ensures staker interests are always protected alongside LDO governance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Lido?
Lido is a liquid staking protocol for Ethereum. It lets you stake ETH and receive stETH, a liquid token that accrues staking rewards daily. You can use stETH across DeFi while your ETH remains staked on the Beacon Chain, earning consensus and execution layer rewards.
What is stETH and how does it work?
stETH (staked ETH) is a rebasing token — your wallet balance automatically updates daily to reflect staking rewards. If you hold 10 stETH today, tomorrow you might hold 10.0008 stETH. No claiming, no compounding — rewards appear as additional tokens in your wallet.
How much ETH do I need to stake?
There is no minimum. You can stake any amount of ETH through Lido. This is a major advantage over solo staking, which requires exactly 32 ETH to operate a validator. Lido pools deposits from many users and distributes them across professional node operators.
What APR does Lido offer?
Lido's staking APR fluctuates based on Ethereum network conditions, typically ranging from 3% to 4% annually. Lido charges a 10% fee on rewards (5% to node operators, 5% to DAO treasury), so stakers receive approximately 90% of gross staking rewards.
Is Lido safe?
Lido's contracts have been audited by multiple firms including Sigma Prime, MixBytes, and Quantstamp. The protocol uses 30+ distributed node operators, has a bug bounty via Immunefi, and has been operational since December 2020 with $14B+ TVL secured.
Can I unstake my ETH from Lido?
Yes. Request a withdrawal through Lido's interface (processes in 1-5 days depending on the exit queue) or swap stETH for ETH instantly on Curve, Uniswap, or other DEXes. Both options are available at any time.
